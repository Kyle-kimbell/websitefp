Who We Are

Local monitoring for farms planning smarter energy work.

We work with North Valley farms that rely on pumps, irrigation, remote systems, cold storage, and field equipment. Our job is to turn vague problems into measured projects a grower can understand, price, and discuss with the right funding or financing partner.

01

Find the load

We look at the equipment and systems that drive the bill: pumps, irrigation controls, wells, tanks, refrigeration, lighting, and always-on devices.

02

Measure the problem

We install or review monitoring that shows runtime, status, water movement, outages, and usage patterns before recommending an upgrade.

03

Document the project

We help organize the plain-English scope: what is being changed, why it matters, what needs monitoring, and what data should be kept.

REAP Angle

Built around energy efficiency and renewable energy readiness.

USDA's Rural Energy for America Program supports eligible agricultural producers and rural small businesses with financing for renewable energy systems and energy efficiency improvements. Application windows and rules change, so we focus on the part we can make clearer: the farm problem, the proposed upgrade, and the monitoring plan behind it.

Step 1

REAP-fit walkthrough

Identify equipment that could be part of an energy efficiency or renewable energy project.

Step 2

Project scope

Define the upgrade, monitoring points, rough owner goals, and the records needed before the farm talks to a grant writer, lender, contractor, or USDA office.

Step 3

Install and track

Keep the system visible after the work starts, so the farm has a clearer record of performance, outages, and operating changes.
